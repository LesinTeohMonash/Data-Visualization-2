Food Emission Produced by Each Food

The line charts above shows the food emission for all foods in the list.

If you want to check the emission of certain food, simply click on the "Food Selection" tab and choose the food that you after.

Observe these four line charts, it is obvious that beef, lamb, milk and food which are non vegan produce higher emission.

On the other hand, plant based food always produce less emission.

From these graphs, we only know which food produce more or less emission compared to other foods.

However, there is no clue to the factors and reasons to what cause the food emission vary to the types of foods.

Food Emission Produced by Each Milk

From the pie charts above, we know that dairy milk produce higher emission to the environment.

Meanwhile, the plant-based milk are always produce less emision to the environment.

From the bar chart, we know the impact that brought by the dairy milk is the most compared to other types of milk.

The type of milk that leads the least impact to the environment is soy milk and it is strongly recommended to consume any plant-based milk instead of dairy milk.

To see less types of milk, press the label on the right of the pie charts and bar chart.

Hover to the graphs and the information will be shown on the tooltip

Share Emission by each Countries

The line chart above shows the food emission produce by each of the country.

For instance, if a food is produced from Australia but exported to Africa, the food emission goes under Australia.

The share here means the percentage of the food which produce n percentage over the total food for each country.

For example, Australia produces n amount of food, while 10% of of them producing food emission. The share of food emission in Australia is 10%

The map above shows which counties produce higher amount of food emission all around the world.

The colour of the countries and the size of the bubbles indicate the amount of the food emission

It is obvious the Brazil produces the highest amount of food emission compared to other countries in the list.

The amount of food emission produced by Australia is not as high as Brazil and China, instead, Australia only produces not greater than 1.2 percent of food emission.

The information of the countries for food emission is included in the tooltop. Simply hover the cursor to the countries you after, and the information will be shown.
